Getting it right · For her

The right fit changes everything.

An incorrect flange size is the most common reason nursing mamas stop pumping early. We want her to get it right the first time. This guide is here to make sure she does.

The Nurture Pro™ · Flange sizing

How to measure. In 60 seconds.

  1. 01

    Measure your nipple

    Use a ruler or soft tape measure. Measure the diameter of your nipple only — not the areola around it. Measure in millimetres.

  2. 02

    Add 3 to 4 millimetres

    This gives you your optimal flange size. For example: nipple diameter 18mm + 3mm = 21mm flange.

  3. 03

    Choose your size

    Match your measurement to the size chart below. Between sizes? Always size up.

Nipple diameter Recommended flange Fits best for
Up to 16mm17mm flangePetite
17 – 18mm19mm flangeSmall
19 – 20mm21mm flangeAverage
21 – 23mm24mm flangeStandard
24mm+27mm flangeLarger

Between sizes? Always size up. A flange that is too small is more uncomfortable than one that is slightly too large.

Signs of correct flange fit

  • ✦Nipple moves freely in the tunnel with no rubbing
  • ✦No areola tissue is being pulled in
  • ✦No pain or discomfort during pumping
  • ✦Full milk expression in 15 – 20 minutes

Signs the flange is too small

  • ✦Nipple rubs the sides of the tunnel
  • ✦Pain or pinching during pumping
  • ✦White ring around the base of the nipple after use
